Truss Boom 4 Critical Safety Rules
Truss booms are essential for moving large or awkward loads, but their safe operation requires strict
attention to crucial safety rules regarding load capacity.


           Know Your Capacity
           Truss booms are rated LESS than the telehandler's overall capacity. Never assume the machine's
           full capacity applies.



              Use the Attachment Load Chart
              Always use the load chart specific to the truss boom 4 NOT the standard machine chart.



                  Capacity Drops as You Extend
                  Multiple load positions exist. Rated capacity DECREASES the further the load extends
                  from the machine.



                     Suspended Loads
                     Special rules apply. Always refer to the Operator's Manual before handling any
                     suspended load.



       ¦     Overloading a truss boom is a serious safety hazard. When in doubt 4 check the chart.

Class 135 Attachment Types 4 Overview

 Side Shifter
 Shifts forks left or right hydraulically. Most common forklift attachment. No need to reposition the truck.




 Rotator
 Grabs and rotates the load. Used for dumping, mixing, or inverting (e.g., ingredient bins, barrels).




 Carton Clamp
 Pressure clamps lift loads without a pallet. Ideal for appliances and boxed goods.




 Paper Roll Handler
 Specialized clamp for large paper or fabric rolls. Prevents damage during handling.




 Fork Positioner
 Hydraulically adjusts fork spacing for different pallet sizes. Operator stays seated.




 Slipsheet Attachment
 Push/pull mechanism handles loads on flat slipsheets. Eliminates the need for pallets.


      Each attachment requires its own load chart and operator training. Always verify manufacturer approval before use.
