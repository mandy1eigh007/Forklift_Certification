Forklift (Classes 135) &
Telehandler (Class 7) Operator
Training
This training module offers comprehensive guidance for operating forklifts
(Classes 1-5) and telehandlers (Class 7). It covers essential safety,
operational procedures, and regulatory requirements. The module
highlights key similarities and differences in operator training and practical
application, ensuring alignment with industry best practices and standards.

Equipment Overview: Classes 1-5 vs. Class 7
 Feature                Classes 1-5 Forklifts                      Class 7 Telehandlers

 Primary Use            Warehouse & industrial facilities          Construction & outdoor job sites

 Operating Surface      Smooth, level concrete                     Rough, uneven terrain

 Steering               Rear-wheel (tight turns)                   Front-wheel or 4-wheel modes

 Load Handling          Vertical mast with fixed forks             Telescoping boom with attachments

 Visibility Challenge   Elevated loads block forward view          Boom extension creates blind spots

 Capacity Reference     Data plate (fixed capacity)                Load chart (variable by boom position)

Classes 1-5: Instructor Guidelines &
Safety Focus
  Highlighting Key Differences:
  Highlight differences between Classes 1-5 (indoor, smooth floors) and Classes 6-7
  (rough terrain).



  Critical Safety Awareness:
  Emphasize tail swing awareness in tight spaces.



  Warehouse Hazard Discussion:
  Discuss common hazards in warehouse environments (pedestrians, shelving, blind
  corners).



  Practical Demonstrations:
  Provide practical demonstrations of stability triangle principles for Class 1-5 PITs.
Classes 1-5: Key Operating Environments
 Warehouses & Facilities

 Smooth Concrete Floors

 Pedestrian Traffic Zones
 Mixed-use areas demanding constant awareness.



 Indoor Environments
 Enclosed spaces with ventilation and lighting considerations.
